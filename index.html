AI-Powered Travel Planning Project

Turning holiday chaos into a structured, AI-assisted system.
I wasn't trying to use AI. I was trying to stop making decisions. A week-long trip across Sicily, Malta and Crete meant hundreds of tiny choices: Which bus? Which route? Is this attraction actually open? Can we cook dinner here? Each decision only takes a few minutes. Together they become the holiday.

Project Overview

Using Claude, I created two bilingual resources for a 7-day Mediterranean journey:

  • Interactive Travel Field Guide
  • Cultural Companion Booklet
  • Chinese + Swedish versions
  • HTML and PDF formats

The project evolved into a practical experiment in prompt engineering, verification systems, and human-in-the-loop AI workflows.

Lessons Learned

  • Feed reality, not wishes. Screenshots, kitchen photos and transport schedules improved outputs dramatically.
  • Confidence is not correctness. Verification loops caught mistakes before they became real-world problems.
  • Manage AI like a teammate. The best results came after several rounds of feedback and refinement.
  • Leave some things unoptimised. AI planned the buses and ferries. It did not predict travel fatigue, spontaneous detours, or two adults becoming surprisingly passionate about where to eat lunch. Probably for the best.
